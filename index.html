The Routes

of America

The routes and highways around the United States are our guided pathways for where we need to go around America. There are many different types of routes and highways in America like interstate highways, U.S. highways, state highways, and county highways. This website takes you on a road trip down the different types of routes and highways you can discover in America.

Interstate Highways

Interstate Highways are the transportation backbone of the U.S. with millions of Americans driving on them for commutes, long-distance travel, freight transport daily, etc. These types of highways are designed to maximize high-speed travel safety and efficiency. Click on the button down below to learn more about these backbone highways.





U.S. Highways

U.S. Highways are older highway routes consisting of mostly surface-level trunk roads. These types of highways have been relegated to regional and intrastate traffic, as they've been largely supplanted by the Interstate system for long-distance travel. Click on the button down below to learn more about these historic routes.





State Routes

Every state in America has a state highway system. State highways are often designed after their corresponding state with their state's geometric shape or symbol such as its flag. Click on the button down below to learn more about the different types of state routes in America.
